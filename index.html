What is LLM?

LLM stands for Large Language Model — an AI system trained on massive text datasets to understand and generate human language.

LLMs learn by predicting the next token (word or character) in a sequence. Through billions of these predictions, they develop an understanding of grammar, facts, and reasoning.

Well-known examples include GPT-4 by OpenAI and Claude by Anthropic.

What is a Token?

A token is a small chunk of text that an LLM processes at once — roughly 3-4 characters or about ¾ of a word on average. Words, punctuation, and even spaces can each become separate tokens.

Tokens matter because they are the basic unit LLMs use to measure context window size (how much text the model can "see" at once) and pricing (APIs charge per token consumed).

For example, the sentence "Hello, world!" splits into 4 tokens: "Hello", ",", "world", "!"

Language also affects token count. English is efficient — one word is often one token. Azerbaijani, however, is an agglutinative language with long compound words, so the same meaning typically requires more tokens. For example, the English phrase "I cannot come" is 4 tokens, while its Azerbaijani equivalent "Gələ bilmərəm" may be split into more tokens because the model's vocabulary covers Azerbaijani less thoroughly.
